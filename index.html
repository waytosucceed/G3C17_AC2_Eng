Prepositions


Convention/Grammar

Prepositions are short words that show the relationship between other words in a sentence.

They indicate location, time, direction, or other connections. Common prepositions include in, on, at, by, with, from, to, and many others.

Prepositional phrases are groups of words that begin with a preposition and end with a noun or pronoun.

The prepositional phrase functions as a single unit, providing additional details about the noun or pronoun it modifies.

Prepositional phrases can appear at the beginning or end of a sentence.



Words that I learnt from the story

Mischief: Playful or troublesome behaviour that causes minor harm or annoyance.
Sentence: The kids were up to mischief when they played pranks on their neighbours.

Curiosity: A strong desire to learn or know something.
Sentence: The curious kitten couldn't resist exploring the new toy.

Extravagant: Excessively expensive or going beyond what is reasonable or appropriate.
Sentence: The extravagant party had decorations, food, and entertainment that cost a lot of money.
